Counseling (Management Concentration)

商务咨询学硕士（国际学生管理方向）

The Pillar College Master of Arts in Counseling Degree Program contains a concentration specifically created for the Business Professional with a global reach and for those seeking to gain an understanding of human development, organizational behavior, team dynamics, motivation, and performance as well as effective human resource management.

In addition to graduate courses in counseling such as Counseling Theory and Practice, Human Growth and Development, Ethical and Legal Issues in Counseling (to name a few), courses in Management have been crafted into a competitive concentration to include Human Resource Management, Conflict Management, Cross-Cultural Organizational Management, and Applied Organizational Psychology. This option is for the management practitioner who is not seeking licensure, nor pursuing a career in clinical counseling in the state of New Jersey.

Graduates of the program will gain knowledge and experience that is immediately accessible to many sectors and be able to pursue careers in Human Resource Management, Customer Service, Management and Marketing Analysis, Labor Relations, and Industrial Psychology.

Core Courses Credits (45 credit hours)

  • COUN 520 Counseling Theory and Practice 3
  • COUN 530 Research and Program Evaluation 3
  • COUN 550 Counseling Skills 3
  • COUN 560 Social and Cultural Counseling Foundations 3
  • COUN 570 Human Growth and Development 3
  • COUN 580 Orientation to Professional Counseling 3
  • COUN 590 Group Process and Practice 3
  • COUN 595 Group Leadership and Practice 3
  • COUN 610 Psychological Testing and Assessment 3
  • COUN 650 Career Development 3
  • COUN 620 Marriage and Family Counseling 3
  • COUN 630A Mental Health Counseling 3
  • COUN 660A Diagnosis and Treatment Planning 3
  • COUN 680A Professional Internship 3
  • COUN 680A Professional Internship 3

Management Concentration Credits (15 credit hours)

  • COUN 540 Ethical and Legal Implications in Counseling 3
  • MGMT 525 Conflict Management 3
  • ORGL 520/PSYC Applied Organizational Psychology 3
  • MGMT 520 Cross-Cultural Organizational Management 3
  • MGMT 540 Human Resource Management 3
